Image A – 1920 × 1080 Crop

For this assignment I photographed several scenes using the DSLR camera and focused on composition techniques discussed in lecture. I selected this image because it creates visual interest through the rule of thirds and the use of leading lines. The subject is placed slightly off center so the image feels balanced but not static. I also tried to capture depth by including elements in the foreground, middle ground, and background. This layering creates a stronger sense of space and helps guide the viewer’s eye through the frame. After selecting the image, I cropped it to the required 1920 by 1080 pixel dimension to emphasize the horizontal composition. To improve the tonal quality of the photograph I used adjustment layers rather than editing the pixels directly. First I applied a Levels adjustment layer to control the shadow and highlight values and to expand the overall contrast range. Adjusting the black and white points helped the image feel more dynamic and prevented the tones from looking flat. After that I used a Curves adjustment layer to fine tune the midtones and create a gentle S shaped curve, which increased contrast while preserving detail in the lighter areas of the image. These non destructive edits allowed me to experiment with different tonal balances without permanently altering the original file. The final image has clearer contrast and a more defined tonal range while maintaining the natural lighting of the scene.

Image B – 5 × 7 Crop

The second photograph was chosen because it works well in a vertical composition and benefits from a tighter crop. While reviewing the images I took during the photo shoot, this one stood out because the subject naturally draws the viewer’s attention. Cropping the image to a 5 by 7 ratio allowed me to remove distracting areas and focus more directly on the main subject. This crop strengthens the balance of the composition and creates a more intentional framing. I also looked for contrast between shapes and tones so the subject would stand out clearly from the background. For tonal adjustments I again used non destructive adjustment layers. I began with a Levels adjustment to refine the overall brightness and ensure that the darkest areas of the image still contained visible detail. After adjusting the highlights and shadows, I used Curves to subtly increase contrast and refine the tonal balance. This helped separate the subject from the background and improved the overall clarity of the image. Working with adjustment layers allowed me to compare the edited image with the original and evaluate how the tonal changes improved the photograph. Through cropping and tonal adjustments the final result emphasizes composition and lighting while maintaining the original visual qualities captured by the camera.
