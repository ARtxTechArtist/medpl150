Reading 1: "In Plato’s Cave" – Susan Sontag

In “In Plato’s Cave,” Susan Sontag explores the power of photography and how image-making shapes the way we understand reality. She argues that photographs are not neutral records of the world but are framed, selected, and interpreted representations. I was especially struck by her idea that taking a photograph can feel like an act of control. In today’s digital culture, we constantly document our lives, but we rarely question how that documentation changes our experience of reality.

Sontag suggests that photography can distance us from direct experience. Instead of fully engaging with the world, we often experience events through the lens of a camera. This made me reflect on how often I prioritize capturing an image over being present in the moment. At the same time, photography can also preserve memory and create powerful social commentary. In my own work, I want to be more intentional about how I frame and present images, thinking critically about what is included, excluded, and emphasized.
